STI Aware Site Plan Document

Site Name

"STI Aware"
The reason given is that this will be an "easy-to-use website that helps people learn about Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)."

Site Purpose

The site purpose lists multiple objectives that describe the services and information to be provided:

  • Share Clear Information – Help people understand STIs and make smart health choices.
  • Encourage Prevention – Teach ways to stay safe, like using protection and getting vaccines.
  • Reduce Fear & Shame – Help people feel comfortable discussing sexual health without judgment.
  • Support Testing & Treatment – Show why testing early is important and where to get help.

Scenarios

  • Scenario 1: A young adult, Alex, who recently became sexually active, feels confused about the different types of STIs they've heard about. They are looking for a reliable and easy-to-understand source of information to learn about symptoms, risks, and how to protect themselves without feeling judged or embarrassed.
  • Scenario 2: Imani suspects they might have been exposed to an STI but feels nervous and unsure about getting tested. They are looking for information on where they can get tested confidentially in their area and what the testing process involves. They also want to understand what their options are if they test positive.

Color Schema

  • Primary Color: Ash (#B2BEB5) - To be used for main headings, key navigation elements, and accents.
  • Secondary Color: Black (#000000) - To be used for primary body text and strong visual elements.
  • Background Color: White (or Whitesmoke) (#F5F5F5) - To be used as the main background for readability.
  • Accent Color: Black (#000000) - Can also be used for emphasis, such as button text or dividers.

Color Schema Use in Document

The colors listed above (Ash, Black, White/Whitesmoke) are used to style this site plan document itself to provide a visual representation of the intended website design.

Typography Named and Used

  • Primary Font: Satoshi
  • Secondary Font: Space Grotesk (Applied to the main body text and general elements to ensure readability.)

These fonts are used throughout this site plan document to demonstrate the intended typography for the website.
